# Proportion (Direct & Inverse) 🟡

## 📌 What is Proportion?
If two ratios are **equal**, they are said to be in proportion.
```
a : b :: c : d  means  a/b = c/d
```
Read as "a is to b as c is to d"

---

## 📝 All Formulas & Properties

### Fundamental Property
```
Product of Extremes = Product of Means
a × d = b × c
```
Where a, d are extremes and b, c are means.

### Types of Proportion

#### 1. Mean Proportional
Mean proportional of a and c:
```
b = √(a × c)
```
(a, b, c are in continued proportion: a/b = b/c)

#### 2. Third Proportional
Third proportional of a and b:
```
c = b² / a
```
(a, b, c are in continued proportion: a/b = b/c)

#### 3. Fourth Proportional
Fourth proportional of a, b, c:
```
d = (b × c) / a
```
(a:b :: c:d)

---

## 📝 Types of Variation

### Direct Proportion (Direct Variation)
When one quantity **increases**, the other also **increases** at the same rate.
```
a ∝ b  →  a/b = constant  →  a₁/b₁ = a₂/b₂
```
**Examples**: More items → more cost; More distance → more time (at same speed)

### Inverse Proportion (Inverse Variation)
When one quantity **increases**, the other **decreases** at the same rate.
```
a ∝ 1/b  →  a × b = constant  →  a₁ × b₁ = a₂ × b₂
```
**Examples**: More speed → less time; More workers → fewer days

---

## 📝 Componendo & Dividendo

If a/b = c/d, then:

| Rule | Formula |
|------|---------|
| **Componendo** | (a+b)/b = (c+d)/d |
| **Dividendo** | (a-b)/b = (c-d)/d |
| **Componendo & Dividendo** | **(a+b)/(a-b) = (c+d)/(c-d)** |
| **Alternendo** | a/c = b/d |
| **Invertendo** | b/a = d/c |

---

## 📝 Key Properties of Proportion

| Property | If a/b = c/d = k, then... |
|----------|---------------------------|
| Sum property | (a+c)/(b+d) = k |
| Difference property | (a-c)/(b-d) = k |
| a = bk, c = dk | Express in terms of k |

---

## ⚡ Tricks & Shortcuts

> 💡 **Trick 1 – Cross Multiply**: To find any unknown in a:b :: c:d, use ad = bc.

> 💡 **Trick 2 – Mean Proportional Quick**: Mean proportional of two numbers = √(product).
> - Mean proportional of 4 and 9 = √36 = **6**

> 💡 **Trick 3 – Identifying Direct vs Inverse**: Ask yourself: "If one increases, does the other increase or decrease?"
> - Same direction → Direct
> - Opposite direction → Inverse

> 💡 **Trick 4 – Componendo-Dividendo**: Very powerful for equations of the form (a+b)/(a-b) = k. Can solve in one step.

## 📖 Solved Examples

### Example 1: Fourth Proportional
**Q**: Find the fourth proportional to 4, 9, and 12.  
**Solution**:  
- 4:9 :: 12:x → 4x = 9×12 = 108 → x = **27**

---

### Example 2: Mean Proportional
**Q**: Find the mean proportional of 3 and 27.  
**Solution**:  
- Mean = √(3 × 27) = √81 = **9**
- Verify: 3:9 :: 9:27 → 3/9 = 9/27 = 1/3 ✅

---

### Example 3: Third Proportional
**Q**: Find the third proportional to 8 and 12.  
**Solution**:  
- c = 12²/8 = 144/8 = **18**
- Verify: 8:12 :: 12:18 → 8/12 = 12/18 = 2/3 ✅

---

### Example 4: Direct Proportion
**Q**: If 5 pens cost ₹75, how much do 12 pens cost?  
**Solution**:  
- Direct proportion: 5/75 = 12/x
- x = 75 × 12/5 = **₹180**

---

### Example 5: Inverse Proportion
**Q**: 15 workers can build a wall in 48 hours. How many workers needed to build it in 30 hours?  
**Solution**:  
- Inverse proportion: 15 × 48 = x × 30
- x = 720/30 = **24 workers**

---

### Example 6: Componendo-Dividendo
**Q**: If (a+b)/(a-b) = 7/3, find a:b.  
**Solution**:  
Using componendo-dividendo in reverse:
- a/b = (7+3)/(7-3) = 10/4 = **5:2**

---

### Example 7: Word Problem
**Q**: A car travels 240 km in 4 hours. How far will it travel in 7 hours at the same speed?  
**Solution**:  
- Direct proportion: 240/4 = x/7
- x = 240 × 7/4 = **420 km**

---

## 🎯 Exam Tips
- For proportion, always set up the equation and cross multiply
- Direct vs Inverse: this is the foundation for Time & Work and Speed problems
- Componendo-Dividendo is a shortcut hero — saves 3-4 steps in ratio problems
