# Average – Complete Formula Sheet (Aptitude)

> Covers almost all Average questions asked in TCS, Infosys, Accenture, Capgemini, Cognizant, Wipro, SSC, Banking, Railways, and other aptitude exams.

---

# 1. Basic Average ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
Average = Sum of Observations / Number of Observations
```

### Example

```text
Numbers = 10, 20, 30

Average = (10 + 20 + 30) / 3

= 60 / 3

= 20
```

---

# 2. Sum from Average ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
Sum = Average × Number of Observations
```

### Example

```text
Average = 25

Number of Students = 12

Sum = 25 × 12

= 300
```

---

# 3. Number of Observations ⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
Number of Observations

= Sum / Average
```

### Example

```text
Sum = 240

Average = 20

Number = 240 / 20

= 12
```

---

# 4. New Average After Adding One Number ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
New Average

= (Old Sum + New Number) / (Old Number of Observations + 1)
```

### Example

```text
Average = 30

Students = 5

New Student = 40

Old Sum = 30 × 5 = 150

New Average

= (150 + 40) / 6

= 190 / 6

= 31.67
```

---

# 5. New Average After Removing One Number ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
New Average

= (Old Sum − Removed Number)

÷ (Old Number of Observations − 1)
```

### Example

```text
Average = 40

Students = 6

Removed Student = 50

Old Sum = 40 × 6 = 240

New Average

= (240 − 50) / 5

= 38
```

---

# 6. Change in Average When One Value Changes ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
Increase in Average

= Increase in Value

÷ Total Number of Observations
```

Similarly,

```text
Decrease in Average

= Decrease in Value

÷ Total Number of Observations
```

### Example

```text
One student's marks increase by 18.

Total Students = 6

Average increases by

18 / 6

= 3
```

---

# 7. Average of Two Groups ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
Combined Average

= (Sum₁ + Sum₂)

÷ (Number₁ + Number₂)
```

or

```text
Combined Average

= (Average₁ × Number₁ + Average₂ × Number₂)

÷ (Number₁ + Number₂)
```

### Example

```text
Group A

Average = 20

Students = 10

Group B

Average = 30

Students = 20

Combined Average

= (20×10 + 30×20)

÷ (10+20)

= 800 / 30

= 26.67
```

---

# 8. Weighted Average ⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
Weighted Average

= (Value₁×Weight₁ + Value₂×Weight₂ + ...)

÷ (Total Weight)
```

### Example

```text
80 marks with weight 2

60 marks with weight 3

Weighted Average

= (80×2 + 60×3)

÷ 5

= 68
```

---

# 9. Consecutive Numbers Average ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

For consecutive numbers,

```text
Average = Middle Number
```

### Examples

```text
11, 12, 13

Average = 12
```

```text
20, 21, 22, 23, 24

Average = 22
```

---

# 10. Average of First n Natural Numbers ⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
Average

= (n + 1) / 2
```

### Example

```text
First 20 Natural Numbers

Average

= (20 + 1) / 2

= 10.5
```

---

# 11. Average of First n Even Numbers ⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
Average = n + 1
```

### Example

```text
First 10 Even Numbers

Average

= 10 + 1

= 11
```

---

# 12. Average of First n Odd Numbers ⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
Average = n
```

### Example

```text
First 15 Odd Numbers

Average

= 15
```

---

# 13. Average Speed ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula (Equal Distances)

```text
Average Speed

= (2 × Speed₁ × Speed₂)

÷ (Speed₁ + Speed₂)
```

### Example

```text
60 km/h and 40 km/h

Average Speed

= (2×60×40)

÷ (60+40)

= 48 km/h
```

# Quick Tips

- ✅ Always calculate the **Sum** first if Average and Number are given.
- ✅ Remember: **Sum = Average × Number** solves many questions directly.
- ✅ For consecutive numbers, the **middle number is the average**.
- ✅ If one value changes, don't recalculate everything—use the **Change in Average** formula.
- ✅ For two groups, use the **Combined Average** formula instead of averaging the two averages directly.
- ✅ Use the **Average Speed** formula only when **equal distances** are covered.
