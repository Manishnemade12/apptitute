# Basic Work-Efficiency Concept 🔴

## 📌 Core Idea
If A can do a work in **n days**, then A's **one day work (efficiency)** = **1/n** of the total work.

The best approach is the **LCM Method**: Assume Total Work = LCM of all given days.

---

## 📝 All Formulas

### Basic Relationships
```
Work = Efficiency × Time
Time = Work / Efficiency
Efficiency = Work / Time
```

### One Day's Work
```
If A completes work in n days → A's 1 day work = 1/n
```

### LCM Method (Recommended)
1. Total Work = LCM of all given days
2. Efficiency of each = Total Work / Their days
3. Combined Efficiency = Sum of individual efficiencies
4. Time to complete = Total Work / Combined Efficiency

---

## 📝 Key Properties

| Property | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| More efficient = fewer days | Efficiency and time are inversely proportional |
| Efficiencies add up | When people work together |
| Work = 1 (fraction method) | Total work = 1 unit in the traditional method |
| Work done in d days | = d × efficiency |

---

## ⚡ Tricks & Shortcuts

> 💡 **Trick 1 – LCM Method**: Always the fastest. Avoid fractions entirely.
> - A=10 days, B=15 days → Total Work = LCM(10,15) = 30
> - A's eff = 3, B's eff = 2 → Together = 5 → Time = 30/5 = 6 days

> 💡 **Trick 2 – Two People Shortcut**: If A takes 'a' days, B takes 'b' days:
> ```
> Together = (a × b) / (a + b) days
> ```

> 💡 **Trick 3 – Ratio of Efficiency**: If A is twice as efficient as B, and B takes 20 days, then A takes 10 days.
> - Efficiency ratio = inverse of time ratio

---

## 📖 Solved Examples

### Example 1: Basic LCM Method
**Q**: A can do a work in 12 days, B in 18 days. How long together?  
**Solution**:  
- Total Work = LCM(12,18) = 36
- A's eff = 3, B's eff = 2
- Together = 5 per day → Time = 36/5 = **7.2 days** or **7 days 4.8 hours**

---

### Example 2: Two-Person Shortcut
**Q**: A takes 20 days, B takes 30 days. Together?  
**Solution**: (20×30)/(20+30) = 600/50 = **12 days**

---

### Example 3: Partial Work
**Q**: A takes 10 days. He works for 4 days and leaves. How much work remains?  
**Solution**:  
- Work done = 4/10 = 2/5
- Remaining = 1 - 2/5 = **3/5**

---

### Example 4: Sequential Work
**Q**: A takes 20 days, B takes 30 days. A works for 5 days, then B finishes the rest. How many total days?  
**Solution**:  
- Total Work = LCM(20,30) = 60
- A's eff = 3. In 5 days, A does 15 units
- Remaining = 45. B's eff = 2. B takes 45/2 = 22.5 days
- Total = 5 + 22.5 = **27.5 days**

---

### Example 5: Efficiency Ratio
**Q**: A is 3 times as efficient as B. Together they finish in 15 days. How long does B take alone?  
**Solution**:  
- Let B's eff = 1, A's eff = 3
- Together = 4 units/day, Total work = 15 × 4 = 60
- B alone = 60/1 = **60 days**

---

## 🎯 Exam Tips
- **Always use the LCM method** — it eliminates fractions and speeds up solving
- For two people, the (a×b)/(a+b) formula is instant
- Efficiency ratio = inverse of time ratio — key relationship
