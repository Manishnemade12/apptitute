# Work Equivalence (Men-Days-Hours) 🟡

## 📌 Concept
The **MDH (Men × Days × Hours) Rule** is used when work involves different numbers of workers working for different durations.

---

## 📝 Core Formula
```
(M₁ × D₁ × H₁) / W₁ = (M₂ × D₂ × H₂) / W₂
```
Where:
- M = Number of men (or workers)
- D = Number of days
- H = Hours per day
- W = Amount of work done

If the work is the **same** (W₁ = W₂):
```
M₁ × D₁ × H₁ = M₂ × D₂ × H₂
```

### With Efficiency
If workers have different efficiencies (e.g., men, women, children):
```
(M₁ × D₁ × H₁ × E₁) / W₁ = (M₂ × D₂ × H₂ × E₂) / W₂
```

---

## 📝 Key Properties

| Property | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Men & Days are inversely proportional | More men → fewer days (for same work) |
| Men & Work are directly proportional | More men → more work (in same time) |
| Days & Work are directly proportional | More days → more work (with same men) |
| The product M×D×H measures "total effort" | Total man-hours is constant for the same work |

---

## 📝 Efficiency Conversion Table

| Worker Type | Common Ratios |
|-------------|--------------|
| 1 man = 2 women | If m men = w women, then m/w = efficiency ratio |
| 1 man = 3 boys | Adjust accordingly |
| Use the ratio to convert all to one type | Then apply MDH formula |

---

## ⚡ Tricks & Shortcuts

> 💡 **Trick 1 – Convert All to One Unit**: If "3 men = 5 women", convert everything to "women" (or men) first, then use MDH.

> 💡 **Trick 2 – Man-Days**: If hours/day is constant, just compare Man-Days.
> - 10 men, 20 days = 200 man-days of work
> - 25 men → 200/25 = 8 days

> 💡 **Trick 3 – Half the Work = Half the Man-Days**: Scale proportionally.

## 📖 Solved Examples

### Example 1: Basic MDH
**Q**: 10 men can do a work in 20 days working 8 hrs/day. How many men needed to do it in 10 days working 10 hrs/day?  
**Solution**:  
- M₁D₁H₁ = M₂D₂H₂
- 10 × 20 × 8 = M₂ × 10 × 10
- 1600 = 100 × M₂ → M₂ = **16 men**

---

### Example 2: Different Work Amounts
**Q**: 15 men can build a wall 30m long in 12 days. How many men needed to build a 45m wall in 9 days?  
**Solution**:  
- (15 × 12) / 30 = (M₂ × 9) / 45
- 180/30 = 9M₂/45
- 6 = M₂/5 → M₂ = **30 men**

---

### Example 3: Men and Women
**Q**: 3 men or 5 women can do a work in 20 days. How long will 6 men and 5 women take?  
**Solution**:  
- 3M = 5W → 1M = 5/3 W
- 5W do in 20 days → Total = 100 woman-days
- 6M + 5W = 6×(5/3) + 5 = 10 + 5 = 15 woman-units/day
- Time = 100/15 = **6.67 days** = 6 days 16 hrs

---

### Example 4: Finding Work Amount
**Q**: 12 men can do 3 units of work in 10 days. How many units can 8 men do in 15 days?  
**Solution**:  
- (12 × 10) / 3 = (8 × 15) / W₂
- 40 = 120/W₂ → W₂ = **3 units**

---

### Example 5: Boys and Men
**Q**: 4 men and 6 boys can finish in 8 days. 3 men and 7 boys finish in 10 days. How long for 10 boys?  
**Solution**:  
- Total work = (4M+6B)×8 = (3M+7B)×10
- 32M + 48B = 30M + 70B
- 2M = 22B → M = 11B
- Total = (4×11+6)×8 = 50×8 = 400 boy-days
- 10 boys → 400/10 = **40 days**

---

## 🎯 Exam Tips
- MDH is a simple proportionality formula — set it up and cross multiply
- Convert mixed worker types to a single unit first
- These problems look complex but are straightforward once you identify the variables
