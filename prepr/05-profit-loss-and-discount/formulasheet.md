# Profit, Loss & Discount – Complete Formula Sheet (Aptitude)

> Covers almost all Profit, Loss & Discount questions asked in TCS, Infosys, Accenture, Capgemini, Cognizant, Wipro, SSC, Banking, Railways, and other aptitude exams.

---

# Basic Terms ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Cost Price (CP)

```text
The price at which an article is purchased.
```

### Selling Price (SP)

```text
The price at which an article is sold.
```

### Marked Price (MP)

```text
The price printed/tagged on the article before discount.
```

### Discount

```text
Reduction given on the Marked Price.
```

---

# 1. Profit ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
Profit = SP − CP
```

### Example

```text
CP = ₹500
SP = ₹650

Profit = 650 − 500

= ₹150
```

---

# 2. Loss ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
Loss = CP − SP
```

### Example

```text
CP = ₹800
SP = ₹700

Loss = 800 − 700

= ₹100
```

---

# 3. Profit Percentage ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
Profit % = (Profit / CP) × 100
```

or

```text
Profit % = ((SP − CP) / CP) × 100
```

### Example

```text
CP = ₹400
SP = ₹500

Profit % = (100 / 400) × 100

= 25%
```

---

# 4. Loss Percentage ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
Loss % = (Loss / CP) × 100
```

or

```text
Loss % = ((CP − SP) / CP) × 100
```

### Example

```text
CP = ₹600
SP = ₹480

Loss % = (120 / 600) × 100

= 20%
```

---

# 5. Selling Price (Using Profit %) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
SP = CP × (100 + Profit %) / 100
```

### Example

```text
CP = ₹800

Profit = 25%

SP

= 800 × 125 / 100

= ₹1000
```

---

# 6. Selling Price (Using Loss %) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
SP = CP × (100 − Loss %) / 100
```

### Example

```text
CP = ₹500

Loss = 20%

SP

= 500 × 80 / 100

= ₹400
```

---

# 7. Cost Price (Using Profit %) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
CP = (SP × 100)

÷ (100 + Profit %)
```

### Example

```text
SP = ₹720

Profit = 20%

CP

= 720 × 100 / 120

= ₹600
```

---

# 8. Cost Price (Using Loss %) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
CP = (SP × 100)

÷ (100 − Loss %)
```

### Example

```text
SP = ₹560

Loss = 20%

CP

= 560 × 100 / 80

= ₹700
```

---

# 9. Discount ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
Discount = MP − SP
```

### Example

```text
MP = ₹1000

SP = ₹850

Discount = ₹150
```

---

# 10. Discount Percentage ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
Discount %

= (Discount / MP) × 100
```

or

```text
Discount %

= ((MP − SP) / MP) × 100
```

### Example

```text
MP = ₹1200

SP = ₹900

Discount %

= (300 / 1200) × 100

= 25%
```

---

# 11. Selling Price After Discount ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
SP = MP × (100 − Discount %) / 100
```

### Example

```text
MP = ₹1500

Discount = 20%

SP

= 1500 × 80 / 100

= ₹1200
```

---

# 12. Marked Price ⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
MP = (SP × 100)

÷ (100 − Discount %)
```

### Example

```text
SP = ₹960

Discount = 20%

MP

= 960 × 100 / 80

= ₹1200
```

---

# 13. Successive Discounts ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
Net Discount %

= a + b − (ab / 100)
```

### Example

```text
20% and 10%

Net Discount

= 20 + 10 − (20×10)/100

= 28%
```

---

# 14. Successive Profit/Loss ⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
Net Change %

= a + b + (ab / 100)
```

> Use '+' when both are Profit or both are Loss.

### Example

```text
20% Profit

then

10% Profit

Net Profit

= 20 + 10 + (20×10)/100

= 32%
```

---

# 15. Profit on Marked Price ⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
Profit

= SP − CP
```

where

```text
SP = MP × (100 − Discount %) / 100
```

> First calculate **SP after discount**, then calculate Profit/Loss.

# Quick Tips

- ✅ **Profit and Loss are always calculated on Cost Price (CP).**
- ✅ **Discount is always calculated on Marked Price (MP).**
- ✅ Don't confuse **Selling Price (SP)** with **Marked Price (MP)**.
- ✅ In successive discounts, **never add percentages directly**—use the net discount formula.
- ✅ In questions involving both **Discount and Profit**, first calculate **Selling Price after Discount**, then find Profit or Loss.
- ✅ Learn the direct SP and CP formulas—they save a lot of time in aptitude exams.
