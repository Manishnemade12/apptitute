# CP/SP Relations 🔴

## 📌 Advanced Relationships Between Cost Price and Selling Price

---

## 📝 All Formulas

### Two Articles — Finding Overall Profit/Loss
```
Total CP = CP₁ + CP₂
Total SP = SP₁ + SP₂
Overall Profit/Loss = Total SP - Total CP
Overall % = [(Total SP - Total CP) / Total CP] × 100
```

### SP of x articles = CP of y articles
```
If SP of x = CP of y:
   Let CP of 1 article = 1
   CP of y = y
   SP of x = y, so SP of 1 = y/x
   
   If y > x → Profit% = [(y-x)/x] × 100
   If y < x → Loss%  = [(x-y)/x] × 100
```

### When Profit/Loss is on SP (not CP)
Sometimes profit is calculated on SP:
```
If profit = p% on SP:
   Profit on CP = [p / (100 - p)] × 100 %

If loss = l% on SP:
   Loss on CP = [l / (100 + l)] × 100 %
```

### Break-Even Analysis
```
Break-even point: SP = CP (no profit, no loss)
To gain x% after initially losing y%:
   Required selling price = CP × (100 + x) / 100
```

| Given | Find | Formula |
|-------|------|---------|
| CP and Profit% | SP | SP = CP(100+P%)/100 |
| CP and Loss% | SP | SP = CP(100-L%)/100 |
| SP and Profit% | CP | CP = SP×100/(100+P%) |
| SP and Loss% | CP | CP = SP×100/(100-L%) |
| CP₁ at P% profit, CP₂ at L% loss | Overall | Calculate each SP, sum up |

## ⚡ Tricks & Shortcuts

> 💡 **Trick 1 – Ratio Method for "SP of x = CP of y"**:
> - SP:CP = y:x
> - If y > x → Profit, if y < x → Loss
> - %age = |y-x|/x × 100

> 💡 **Trick 2 – Profit on SP to Profit on CP**: If 20% profit on SP → 20/80 × 100 = 25% profit on CP.

> 💡 **Trick 3 – Two Items Sold at Same Price**:
> - One at a% profit, other at b% profit → Total CP = SP × [100/(100+a) + 100/(100+b)]

## 📖 Solved Examples

### Example 1: SP of x = CP of y
**Q**: SP of 5 items = CP of 7 items. Find profit %.  
**Solution**: Profit% = (7-5)/5 × 100 = **40%**

---

### Example 2: Profit on SP
**Q**: A shopkeeper makes 25% profit on SP. Find actual profit % on CP.  
**Solution**: Profit on CP = 25/(100-25) × 100 = 25/75 × 100 = **33.33%**

---

### Example 3: Overall Profit/Loss
**Q**: Article A: CP=500, sold at 20% profit. Article B: CP=300, sold at 10% loss. Overall profit/loss%?  
**Solution**:  
- SP_A = 500 × 1.2 = 600
- SP_B = 300 × 0.9 = 270
- Total CP = 800, Total SP = 870
- Profit = 70, Profit% = 70/800 × 100 = **8.75%**

---

### Example 4: Finding CP from SP and Profit%
**Q**: An article is sold for ₹1380 at 15% profit. Find CP.  
**Solution**: CP = 1380 × 100/115 = **₹1200**

---

### Example 5: Desired Profit After Loss
**Q**: A shopkeeper bought an article for ₹800. At what price should he sell to make 25% profit?  
**Solution**: SP = 800 × 125/100 = **₹1000**

---

## 🎯 Exam Tips
- "Profit on SP" vs "Profit on CP" is a common trap — read the question carefully
- For "SP of x = CP of y" problems, just use the (y-x)/x formula
- Always track which value (CP or SP) your percentage is based on
