# Conditional & Combined Probability 🟢

## 📌 Concepts

### Independent Events
Events where one doesn't affect the other.
```
P(A∩B) = P(A) × P(B)
```

### Dependent Events (Conditional)
```
P(A∩B) = P(A) × P(B|A)
```
Where P(B|A) = probability of B given A has occurred.

### Mutually Exclusive
Events that cannot occur simultaneously.
```
P(A∪B) = P(A) + P(B)    [no overlap]
```

---

## 📖 Solved Examples

### Example 1: Independent
**Q**: A coin and a die are thrown. P(Head AND 6)?  
**Solution**: 1/2 × 1/6 = **1/12**

---

### Example 2: Without Replacement (Dependent)
**Q**: Bag has 5 red, 3 blue balls. 2 drawn without replacement. P(both red)?  
**Solution**: (5/8) × (4/7) = 20/56 = **5/14**

---

### Example 3: At Least One
**Q**: P(at least one 6 in 3 rolls)?  
**Solution**: 1 - P(no 6) = 1 - (5/6)³ = 1 - 125/216 = **91/216**

---

## Quick Review
- Independent: P(A∩B) = P(A)×P(B)
- Dependent: P(A∩B) = P(A)×P(B|A)
- At least one = 1 - P(none)
