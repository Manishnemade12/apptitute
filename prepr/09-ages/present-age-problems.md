# Present Age / Past-Future Age Problems 🟡

## 📌 Concept
Age problems involve finding present, past, or future ages using given relationships. The key insight: **the age difference between two people NEVER changes**.

---

## 📝 Core Formulas

### Basic Relationships
```
Age after n years = Present Age + n
Age n years ago   = Present Age - n
Age difference remains constant regardless of time
```

### Setting Up Equations
- Let present age = x
- "n years ago" = x - n
- "n years hence/later" = x + n
- "twice as old" = 2x, "half as old" = x/2

---

## 📝 Key Properties

| Property | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Age difference is constant | If A is 5 years older than B, this stays true forever |
| Ratio of ages changes with time | Unlike difference, ratio keeps changing |
| Age cannot be negative | Use this to validate answers |
| "Born" = age was 0 at that time | |

---

## ⚡ Tricks & Shortcuts

> 💡 **Trick 1 – Use Variables**: Let the younger person's present age = x. Express everything in terms of x.

> 💡 **Trick 2 – Difference Method**: For ratio-based problems:
> - Present ratio = a:b → ages could be ax and bx → difference = (a-b)x
> - Given age difference → find x immediately

> 💡 **Trick 3 – Work Backwards from Options**: In MCQs, plug in answer choices — fastest method.

---

## 📖 Solved Examples

### Example 1: Basic
**Q**: A's present age is 40. What was his age 8 years ago?  
**Solution**: 40 - 8 = **32 years**

---

### Example 2: Equation-Based
**Q**: A is twice as old as B. 5 years ago, A was 3 times as old as B. Find present ages.  
**Solution**:  
- Let B = x, A = 2x
- 5 years ago: (2x-5) = 3(x-5) → 2x-5 = 3x-15 → x = 10
- B = **10**, A = **20**

---

### Example 3: Sum of Ages
**Q**: Sum of ages of A and B is 50. A is 6 years older than B. Find their ages.  
**Solution**:  
- A + B = 50, A - B = 6
- A = 28, B = 22

---

### Example 4: Future Age
**Q**: Father is 30 years older than son. In 12 years, father will be twice the son's age. Find present ages.  
**Solution**:  
- Let son = x, father = x + 30
- (x+30+12) = 2(x+12) → x+42 = 2x+24 → x = 18
- Son = **18**, Father = **48**

---

### Example 5: Average Age
**Q**: Average age of 5 members of a family is 24. If youngest is 6, average age of remaining is?  
**Solution**: Total = 120. Remaining = 120-6 = 114. Avg = 114/4 = **28.5**

---

## 🎯 Exam Tips
- Age problems are essentially linear equation problems — set up and solve
- The constant difference is the most useful property
- In MCQs, always try plugging in options first
