# Basic Alligation Rule 🟡

## 📌 What is Alligation?
Alligation is a rule to find the **ratio in which two or more ingredients** at different prices (or concentrations) must be mixed to produce a mixture at a given price (or concentration).

---

## 📝 The Alligation Formula (Rule of Alligation)

### Two Ingredients
If two ingredients with values (price/concentration) **C₁** and **C₂** are mixed to get a mixture of value **Cm**:
```
Quantity of C₁ / Quantity of C₂ = (C₂ - Cm) / (Cm - C₁)
```

### Visual Diagram (Cross Method)
```
     C₁              C₂
       \             /
         Cm (Mean)
       /             \
   (C₂ - Cm)     (Cm - C₁)
```
**Ratio = (C₂ - Cm) : (Cm - C₁)**

---

## 📝 Key Properties
| Property | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Cm always lies between C₁ and C₂ | C₁ < Cm < C₂ |
| Closer to the cheaper → more of expensive | And vice versa |
| Works for price, concentration, percentage, speed, etc. | Universal rule |

---

## ⚡ Tricks & Shortcuts

> 💡 **Trick 1 – Cross Difference**: Just take the difference cross-wise. The difference of the mean from each ingredient gives the ratio of the other ingredient.

> 💡 **Trick 2 – Works for Weighted Average Too**: Alligation is just a visual shortcut for the weighted average formula.

---

## 📖 Solved Examples

### Example 1: Price Mixing
**Q**: Rice at ₹20/kg mixed with rice at ₹30/kg to get mixture at ₹24/kg. Ratio?  
**Solution**: (30-24):(24-20) = 6:4 = **3:2**

---

### Example 2: Milk and Water
**Q**: Milk at ₹50/L mixed with water (free, ₹0) to sell at ₹40/L. Ratio of milk to water?  
**Solution**: (0-40):(40-50) = 40:10 = **4:1**

---

### Example 3: Average Problem
**Q**: Group A avg 40, Group B avg 60. Combined avg 45. Ratio of A to B?  
**Solution**: (60-45):(45-40) = 15:5 = **3:1**

---

## 🎯 Exam Tips
- Draw the alligation cross diagram — it prevents sign errors
- This is the fastest method for any mixing/weighted average problem
