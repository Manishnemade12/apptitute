# Mixing Quantities & Replacement Problems 🟡

## 📌 Mixing Two or More Quantities
When quantities at different concentrations or prices are mixed:
```
C₁Q₁ + C₂Q₂ = Cm(Q₁ + Q₂)
```

---

## 📝 Replacement Formula (Milk-Water Type)

### Repeated Replacement
If a container has **V** litres of pure liquid. Each time, **x** litres is taken out and replaced with water:
```
After n operations:
Amount of pure liquid = V × (1 - x/V)^n
Concentration = (1 - x/V)^n × 100 %
```

---

## 📖 Solved Examples

### Example 1: Mixing
**Q**: 20L of 30% solution mixed with 30L of 50% solution. Concentration?  
**Solution**: (20×30 + 30×50) / 50 = (600+1500)/50 = **42%**

---

### Example 2: Replacement
**Q**: 20L milk. 4L removed and replaced with water. Done 3 times. Milk remaining?  
**Solution**: 20 × (1-4/20)³ = 20 × (4/5)³ = 20 × 64/125 = **10.24L**

---

### Example 3: Finding Operations
**Q**: 80L milk. 8L replaced each time. After how many times is milk = 58.32L?  
**Solution**: 80×(1-8/80)^n = 58.32 → (0.9)^n = 0.729 = (0.9)³ → n = **3**

---

## Quick Review
- Mixing: Use weighted average or alligation
- Replacement: V × (1-x/V)^n
- Concentration decreases exponentially with each replacement
