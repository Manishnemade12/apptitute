# Relative Speed 🔴

## 📌 Concept
When two objects are **both moving**, their effective speed relative to each other depends on their **direction**.

---

## 📝 Formulas

### Same Direction (One chasing/overtaking other)
```
Relative Speed = |S₁ - S₂|
```
Objects move apart slowly or one catches up.

### Opposite Direction (Moving towards or away from each other)
```
Relative Speed = S₁ + S₂
```
Objects approach each other quickly.

---

## 📝 Applications

### Meeting Problems
Two people start from points A and B (distance D apart) and move towards each other:
```
Time to meet = D / (S₁ + S₂)
```

### Overtaking Problems
Person A (faster) starts behind person B:
```
Time to overtake = Gap / (S₁ - S₂)
```

### Multiple Meetings
Two people moving between A and B repeatedly:
```
1st meeting: D / (S₁ + S₂) time
2nd meeting (if both turn back): 3D / (S₁ + S₂) time from start
nth meeting: (2n-1) × D / (S₁ + S₂) time from start
```

---

## ⚡ Tricks & Shortcuts

> 💡 **Trick 1 – Direction Determines Operation**: Same → Subtract, Opposite → Add. That's it!

> 💡 **Trick 2 – When They Meet**: Total distance covered by both combined = D (the gap between them). So time = D / relative speed.

> 💡 **Trick 3 – Circular Track**: Two people on a circular track of length L:
> - Same direction: Meet every L/(S₁-S₂) time
> - Opposite direction: Meet every L/(S₁+S₂) time

---

## 📖 Solved Examples

### Example 1: Opposite Direction
**Q**: Two people 150 km apart walk towards each other at 10 and 5 kmph. When do they meet?  
**Solution**: Time = 150/(10+5) = 150/15 = **10 hrs**

---

### Example 2: Same Direction
**Q**: A is 60 km ahead of B. B travels at 80 kmph, A at 60 kmph. When does B catch A?  
**Solution**: Time = 60/(80-60) = 60/20 = **3 hrs**

---

### Example 3: Circular Track
**Q**: A and B run around a 400m track. A at 6 m/s, B at 4 m/s. When do they first meet (starting from same point, same direction)?  
**Solution**: Time = 400/(6-4) = 400/2 = **200 seconds**

---

## 🎯 Exam Tips
- Always clarify direction before applying the formula
- Meeting problems: relative speed = sum (towards each other)
- Overtaking problems: relative speed = difference (same direction)
