# Simple Interest (SI) & Compound Interest (CI) – Complete Formula Sheet (Aptitude)

> Covers almost all Simple Interest & Compound Interest questions asked in TCS, Infosys, Accenture, Capgemini, Cognizant, Wipro, SSC, Banking, Railways, and other aptitude exams.

---

# Basic Terms ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Principal (P)

```text
Original amount borrowed or invested.
```

### Rate of Interest (R)

```text
Interest charged per year (in %).
```

### Time (T)

```text
Time period (generally in years).
```

### Amount (A)

```text
Final Amount = Principal + Interest
```

---

# PART A – SIMPLE INTEREST (SI)

# 1. Simple Interest ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
SI = (P × R × T) / 100
```

### Example

```text
P = ₹5000

R = 10%

T = 2 years

SI

= (5000 × 10 × 2) / 100

= ₹1000
```

---

# 2. Amount (Simple Interest) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
Amount = Principal + Simple Interest
```

or

```text
A = P + SI
```

### Example

```text
Principal = ₹5000

SI = ₹1000

Amount

= ₹6000
```

---

# 3. Principal ⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
P = (SI × 100)

÷ (R × T)
```

### Example

```text
SI = ₹600

R = 12%

T = 2 years

P

= (600 × 100)

÷ (12 × 2)

= ₹2500
```

---

# 4. Rate of Interest ⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
R = (SI × 100)

÷ (P × T)
```

### Example

```text
SI = ₹400

P = ₹2000

T = 4 years

R

= (400 × 100)

÷ (2000 × 4)

= 5%
```

---

# 5. Time ⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
T = (SI × 100)

÷ (P × R)
```

### Example

```text
SI = ₹1200

P = ₹6000

R = 10%

T

= 2 years
```

---

# PART B – COMPOUND INTEREST (CI)

# 6. Amount (Compound Interest) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

0

### Formula

```text
A = P × (1 + R/100)^T
```

### Example

```text
P = ₹10000

R = 10%

T = 2 years

A

= 10000 × (1.10)^2

= ₹12100
```

---

# 7. Compound Interest ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
CI = Amount − Principal
```

or

```text
CI = A − P
```

### Example

```text
Amount = ₹12100

Principal = ₹10000

CI

= ₹2100
```

---

# 8. Compound Amount (Half-Yearly) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
A

= P × (1 + R/200)^(2T)
```

### Example

```text
P = ₹20000

R = 10%

T = 1 year

A

= 20000 × (1.05)^2

= ₹22050
```

---

# 9. Compound Amount (Quarterly) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
A

= P × (1 + R/400)^(4T)
```

---

# 10. Compound Amount (Annually) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
A

= P × (1 + R/100)^T
```

---

# 11. Difference Between CI and SI ⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

For **2 Years**

```text
Difference

= P × (R/100)^2
```

or

```text
Difference

= (P × R²)

÷ 10000
```

### Example

```text
P = ₹10000

R = 10%

Difference

= (10000 × 100)

÷ 10000

= ₹100
```

---

# 12. Difference Between CI and SI (3 Years) ⭐⭐⭐

### Formula

```text
Difference

= P × (R/100)^2 × (3 + R/100)
```

> Mostly useful in Banking exams.

# Quick Tips

- ✅ **Simple Interest is calculated only on the Principal.**
- ✅ **Compound Interest is calculated on the Principal + Previously Earned Interest.**
- ✅ Always remember:
  - **Amount = Principal + Interest**
  - **Interest = Amount − Principal**
- ✅ In CI questions, carefully check whether the interest is compounded **Annually, Half-Yearly, or Quarterly**.
- ✅ The **Difference between CI and SI** is generally asked for **2 years** in aptitude exams.
- ✅ Convert months into years before applying SI formulas unless the question specifies otherwise.
